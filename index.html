    Welcome!

    This is the personal website of Hannah Haberkern

    I’m a neuroscientist interested in understanding animal behavior, in particular in the context of navigation. If I’m not in the lab or analyzing data, I like to sew or observe animals. You can learn more about my sewing adventures on my blog, or on my more frequently updated Instagram page. If you are interested in my programing projects, check out Github.

    For my publication record go to Publications and for my professional profile visit LinkedIn.


    Sewing and Quilting

    Many of my favorite childhood memories revolve around designing and sewing garments. Recently I rediscovered sewing as a compensatory activity to research. It was then that I stumbled across and instantly fell in love with modern quilting, which opened up a new world of playing with graphic design and fabrics.

    Research

    I am interested in understanding how animals navigate through space. Currently I investigate how (visually guided) navigation in walking fruit flies can change based on past experiences and current context. Previous projects involved different aspects of navigation in adult and larval fruit flies as well as crickets.

    Living with a chronic illness

    Shortly after submitting my PhD thesis I got diagnosed with a systemic autoimmune disease, Sjogrens Syndrom. Occasionally I share experiences related to having a chronic illness on my (sewing) blog.
